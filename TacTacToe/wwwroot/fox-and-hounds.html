🦊 Fox and Hounds — Rules & Scoring

Overview

Fox and Hounds is a classic asymmetric strategy game for 2 players played on the dark squares of an 8×8 checkerboard.

Setup

  • The Fox 🦊 starts on any dark square on the top row (row 0).
  • Four Hounds 🐕 start on the four dark squares of the bottom row (row 7).
  • Roles are randomly assigned — one player gets the Fox, the other controls all four Hounds.

Movement

  • Fox: Moves diagonally in any direction (forward or backward) one square at a time.
  • Hounds: Each hound moves diagonally forward only (toward row 0). No jumping over pieces.
  • The Fox moves first. Players alternate turns.

Winning

  • Fox wins if it reaches the hound back-rank (row 7) or if the Hounds have no legal moves.
  • Hounds win if they trap the Fox so it has no legal moves.

Scoring

  • Fox score: 10 × row reached + 100 bonus for winning.
  • Hounds score: 10 × (7 − fox row) + number of moves made + 100 bonus for winning.

Strategy Tips

  • Fox: Try to find a gap between hounds and dart through — think ahead two moves.
  • Hounds: Advance in a diagonal wall to cut off the Fox's escape routes.
  • Fox: Backward moves can bait hounds into overextending.
  • Use the 💡 Hint button when stuck!
